Meditation: Mental Training

How did the 'process over product' concept affect the way you tackled the site redesign and rebuild?

I feel like the process over product concept should be a no brainer. We're here to learn, not to create the perfect website. BUT... I'm also a bit of a perfectionist, so I'm glad that I was reminded of the "process over product" concept in this case.

In all honesty, I spent 3 times the number of hours I should have on this assignment, but I'm glad I did. I did not focus too much on what the website looked like, but on understanding how to implement the functionalities I had chosen. And I had a good experience doing it.

Working on this website, I have learned a lot, played a lot, been frustrated, had hurray moments, felt great and felt discouraged at times. I gave it a go, and I was kind to myself in the process.

What did you think about meditation before reading chapter 2?

I've started looking into meditation since I decided to register for Dev Academy. Before that though, I thought meditation was some hippie trend that no scientific minded person would have an interest in (and I consider myself part of this category).

Since I've started looking into it, I've been intrigued.

Since I've started reading Search Inside Yourself, I feel glad some other scientific minded people have looked into this and wrote an accessible book about it. I feel it's ok to be intrigued - who cares if anyone thinks I'm a hippie, right?

What new things have you learnt about meditation?

For me, the most useful piece of information in this chapter is the guidelines on how to deal with self-criticism during meditation. The grandmother analogy does not speak to me, but I have a loving partner and can well imagine adopting his state of mind towards me.

Did any of the suggested meditation techniques stand out to you?

Two pieces of technique stand out to me: being relaxed and alert at the same time, and posture.

As a couple dancer, I've spent some time working on my posture. The posture technique I have learned for couple dancing is very similar to the one given in the book. Also, being relaxed but alert reminds me of the state of mind of a follower: relaxed in your body in order to be able to be lead easily, and very attentive and reactive to what your partner does.

Any other musings?

I like the view that meditation is just mental training. Who wouldn't want to do it?

I'm not sure yet how I feel about the claim that "Hapiness is the default state of mind".
