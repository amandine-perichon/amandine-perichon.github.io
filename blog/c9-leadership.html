Leadership

What is your experience of compassionate (or not) leadership?

Based on my personal experience of leading and following, and my own personal beliefs, I strongly believe that compassionate leadership is the most effective leadership. I've experienced working for a manager that had no compassion whatsoever and who thought being a jerk would bring better result, and I found that to be completely untrue, the team was demotivated in pain and turnover was extremly high.

Do the multiplying goodness meditation. How did you find it?

This felt really similar to the loving kindness meditation.

Do the Tonglen meditation. How did you find it?

Breathing in your own suffering felt beneficial compared to simply reflecting on what is going on in your life that might cause you suffering. I believe this is because there is no space for judgment, no "but" or no "did I deserve it?". Just breathe in and breathe out.

How does the SCARF model apply to your Phase 0 experience so far? How will it apply to bootcamp?

Status: Improving my technical skills during Phase 0 has definitely been rewarding. I don't believe there were any status threats. We've had limited interactions in a group, ouside of pair programming which was a positive experience for me. During the bootcamp, our interactions in a group setting might bring up some status threats. Certainty: I definitely felt a lot of uncertainty before registering for the bootcamp, trying to figure out what my next step in life would be.I currently do no feel any uncertainty towards the bootcamp as I believe that this is exactly were I should be and what I should be doing. This feeling is unlikely to change during the bootcamp. The big unknown though is what I will be doing after the bootcamp, and I feel that my anxiety towards this is only going to grow. Autonomy: The EDA experience is helping out greatly with the efficiency of my learning but I still feel that I am in charge of my own learning. I just have a lot of help to achieve my goal. As such I feel a great sense of autonomy. Relatedness: I've met most of our cohort members by now and have had pleasant interactions with them. I'm hoping it will get even better, and that we'll forge a strong bond over the coming weeks. Fairness: I have a lot of support from my partner, I'm very lucky, but I suppose sometimes it feels I have an unfair advantage.

Have a difficult conversation. How did it go?

I've had a few tough conversations with clients and team members while working as a project manager. I've always tried to put myself in the other person's shoes and reflect on the situation from a third person point of view on a content and feeling point of view, less so on an identity level.
